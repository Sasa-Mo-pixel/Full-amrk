Schizophrenia

Introduction:

Schizophrenia is a clinical syndrome of complex, confusing, and debilitating symptoms. It is a severe, chronic, and disabling brain disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves.

Clinical Features (Symptoms):

Positive Symptoms:

  • Hallucinations: False sensory perceptions (Auditory, Visual, Olfactory).
  • Delusions: Fixed false beliefs (Persecutory, Grandiose).
  • Disorganized thinking: Loose associations and speech.

Negative Symptoms:

  • Affective flattening: Reduced range of emotional expression.
  • Alogia: Poverty of speech.
  • Avolition: Lack of motivation.
  • Anhedonia: Inability to feel pleasure.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Observe the client's behavior and listen for auditory hallucinations.
  • Do not argue with the client's delusions. State that you do not share the belief but understand it is real to them.
  • Maintain a safe environment and identify triggers for agitation.
  • Promote social skills and encourage participation in unit activities.

الأعراض السريرية:

الأعراض الإيجابية:

  • الهلاوس: تصورات حسية خاطئة (سمعية، بصرية، شمية).
  • الضلالات: معتقدات خاطئة ثابتة (ضلالات الاضطهاد، العظمة).

الأعراض السلبية:

  • تسطح العاطفة: نقص في التعبير العاطفي.
  • فقدان الإرادة: نقص الدافع والنشاط.
